# Project_ESG_performance_analysis
Simulating a CSRD-compliant ESG performance analysis for a fictional European manufacturer based on the ESG &amp; Financial Performance Dataset from Kaggle. Full analytics pipeline (raw data -> python -> SQL -> Power BI -> PDF report).

## Background & Motivation
Since January 2025 the EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) requires large EUropean companies to publish detailed sustainability reports structured around the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS). This applies to thousands of German companies and demands professionals who can bridge data analytics and ESG reporting. <br>
This project demonstrates how aggregated ESG and financial data can be analysed, benchmarked and visualised in line with ESRS reporting dimensions, covering climate, resource use, workforce and governance. <br>
Rather that simulating a full CSRD report, this project focuses on the analytical layer: trend analysis, industry benchmarking, ESG-financial correlations and emission trajectory forecasting. <br>
The fictional company Lauschke GmbH, a mid sized European maufacturer, serves as the analytical focus entity, derived from  the ESG &amp; Financial Performance Dataset.

## Research Questions
#### Q1 Absolute vs intensity based emission reduction
Has Lauschke GmbH reduced uts CO2 emissions, or does the decline merely reflect falling revenues? CSRD requires both an absolute and intensity-based disclosures.
#### Q2 Industry benchmarking
How does the company's ESG performance compare to European manufacturers? Where are the gaps and which dimensions require the most attention?
#### Q3 Double materiality
Is there a measurable relationship between  ESG performance and financial outcomes over the 2015 - 2025 period? This reflects the CSRD concept of double materiality.
#### Q4 2030 target trajectory
Based on the observed emissions trend, is the company on track to reach a hypothetical 60% CO2 reduction by 2023?
